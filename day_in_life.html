The Galveston Serpant, Captain Jeanette Valdez's Log

September 22nd, 1816

Annotated by First-Mate Rowland Nolte

  • Noon-Time
  • Evenin'
  • Loot Count

Mornin'

  • 0500-- Wake with the sun to rouse the day crew, time allotted for food.
  • (Wake the first mate, have them rouse the day crew with a kitchen pot and ladel. Push hardtack and cheese on them.)
  • 0600-- Relieve night-watch, guncrew and evening nav-crew. Day crew takes control.
  • (First mate Herd's night crew into kitchen for cook to dish out stew, send to bunks.)
  • 0700-- Roll out external sweeps, Raise the masts, and man the winches.
  • (Captain exits cockpit, takes over steering.)
  • 1000-- Observe re-linal waxing of sails, check riging, set gun-crew to general repairs.
  • (Captain embelishes on walking cross-bridge when she is tired of helm-work. )

Noon' Time

  • Hark, On the horizon! A passage of interest!
  • 1200-- A Mast appears from the north, flags mark it as a Scandanavian ship. The size implies a transport vessel. Night and day crew are gathered to man the battalions.
  • 0100-- The ship appears to be turning course, we set ours to follow theres, roll external sweeps toward the north and set chase.
  • 0200-- The Serpant is astride the ship, firing at will, we throw the planks out and the gun-crew boards, capturing who they can, injuring who they can't.
  • 3 men injured on our end, 5 on their's thus far.
  • 0300-- The ship (Marina's Sun) has been successfully over taken. None of the serpants crew were lost only 4 injured- 1 dead, 6 wounded from the Marina's Crew. I find the captain, ludvik Hastain hogtied in the bow.
  • Lewin took a musket ball to the shoulder, Clemence, a knife wound in the side, Rollo has a 2-inch deep gash to the arm and back, Phillips, a musket ball to the leg. I set about treating their wounds with the boatswain.
  • 0300-- Marina's Sun appears to be under a lumber supplier/trader, bringing ship paneling, and iron work into the queen's land. The Sun's urgent-injured crewman are treated using their supplies after our men are fully treated. We do not kill when we need not.
  • 0400-- 3/4ths of their trades are hoisted while the sun's crew watch, we leave their food, but take 2/3rds of the captains chest, filled with coin to purchase goods.
  • Captain is kind with our thievery.
  • Rowland sullies the transcript with lies.
  • 0500-- Our Crew is finished Hauling, we cut the rope of their Barrelman at gun point, they may untie the others when they reach 4 leagues away. Any shorter and our cannon takes the base of their ship.
  • For Posterity: The snake does not carry a cannon. Captain claims them to be cumbersome.

Evenin'

  • 0600-- Ww are 3 naughts away, our guncrew stays aimed at the Sun. We continue to treat the injured. Night crew and injured are sent to barracks to rest.
  • Day crew are rowdy at having to clean up the mess on their own, but night crew have not rested and all respect the captain's orders.
  • 0700-- Food is sent to the barracks and deck, cured meat, cheese, bread and ale.
  • Rousing songs of victory are sung.
  • 0800-- Crew set's about repearing the main damage using curing paste and plaster. Sails are taken down individually for sewing 3, 4, 2, and 2 musket-tears respectively.
  • 0900-- Guns are reloaded, and the haul is positioned securely in the helm.
  • 1000-- Captain and First Mate add booty to the running catalog, reset course direction to port relative to value.
  • The Serpant slithers southward toward the Morrocan ports. To increase our value, we will trade where compensation is the highest and fetch a pretty
    • penny for the spices gained back in the northern ports.
  • 1100-- Captain retires to cabin, day crew is sent to barracks, and night crew returns for work.
